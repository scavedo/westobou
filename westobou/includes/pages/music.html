Johnny Swim


JOHNNY SWIM, a folk-soul bluesy duo comprised of Abner Ramirez and wife Amanda Sudano, daughter of the late Disco Queen Donna Summer will captivate attendees during Westobou’s signature musical event on the historic Parade Grounds at the Old Academy of Richmond County. The duo, described as a hybrid of Lauryn Hill and John Mayer have recently garnered national attention with the release of their latest album Heart Beats, in addition to the first ever position as VH1's You Oughta Know: Artists On the Rise. Such an accolade has aided in driving their new releases to respectable positions on the Billboard charts and appearances on Late Night with Jay Leno, The Today Show and Spoleto Festival USA.


8P Oct 3, 2013 at the Old ARC

T. Hardy Morris


Dead Confederate front man T. Hardy Morris is slated to take the Westobou Stage for a free concert at the Parade Grounds at the Old Academy of Richmond County. Singer/songwriter Ruby the Rabbitfoot will be opening for him.
On July 30th, Hardy released his debut solo album Audition Tapes and completed a coveted summer position as artist in resident on Sirius-XM’s The Loft. While recording his album, Hardy began pursuing a project in collaboration with acclaimed filmmaker Jason Thrasher. The project, Places In Peril, is a musical-film series that investigates abandoned historical landmarks throughout the state of Georgia. Screenings of Thrasher and Morris’ Places In Peril videos will be shown at the Klacsmann Gallery at the Old Academy of Richmond County during gallery hours.

8P Oct 4, 2013 at the Old ARC
Event is Free to the public!!
